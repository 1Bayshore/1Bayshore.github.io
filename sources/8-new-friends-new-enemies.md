"Yeah, I'm so sorry about that," Juþea said. "I wish- wait, sorry, I think there's enough of a gap for us to cross."

"Anyway," Juþea said, "I really am sorry your parents are so scared of arguing they can't stand up for you. I wish there was more I could do to help."

Juþea smiled. "Thanks, Zešemkon," she said. "You really are sweet."

"But it's true," Zešemkon said. "Living with my parents is fine when my grandparents and sister aren't visiting."

"I'm sorry," Juþea said. "It just upsets me that people can be so mean to you, and I want to help, even when it's not necessary."

"It's okay," Zešemkon said. "I want to scare the people in Vlojtum who are mean to you by being translucent or floating over them while bleeding all over, even though that wouldn't help."

Juþea chuckled. "It's fun to fantasise about though, isn't it?" she said.

"It is," Zešemkon said, smiling. "I like to imagine laying in a clearing with flowers growing in me and scaring annoying people when I start moving."

"That's a great image," Juþea said. "You could make a whole story from that."

"Thanks," Zešemkon said. "I don't know exactly what the story would be though."

"Many things would work," Juþea said. "Like a variation on *The Emerald of Life*."
