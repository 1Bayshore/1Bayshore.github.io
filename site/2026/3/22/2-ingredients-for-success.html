2: Ingredients for success

22 March 2026
Previous Next

Zešemkon stood behind the small wooden counter in a corner of the grocery. A metal cash register stood on the counter in front of her, along with a small scale. A couple of bins of apples and a few pears filled the space out from the counter to the other wall, while three rows of shelves stood to the left of the bins, stretching back towards the bins of vegetables at the back. The shop was quiet and still. Zešemkon fidgeted with the buttons on the cash register, tapping them one by one without pressing them down.

A man wearing a blue shirt suddenly opened the door and walked in. He grabbed a shopping basket from next to the door and began to walk through the shop, scanning all of the shelves as he walked. He slowly began to grab items and put them in the basket, starting with a tin of tomato paste, then adding two red potatoes, a bottle of cream, and a bag of salðol cheese. He then began to walk through the shop once more. Zešemkon glanced up from the counter occasionally as he walked through the shop, and started to run her hands along her grey linen dress instead of fidgeting with the cash register buttons.

After walking around the shop a second time, the man in blue walked over to Zešemkon. She glanced up at him and then went back to staring at the counter. "Pardon me," the man said, "I'm visiting some friends in town, and I offered to make dinner this evening, and I decided to make a Wiþvada cream potato pie, but I couldn't find a few things that I need. Could you help me find coriadner?"

"The shelf along the left wall," Zešemkon said quietly. "At the very back."

"Thank you," the man said. He walked over to the shelf along the left wall, scanning it as he got close to the back. After a moment he returned to the counter.

"My apologies," the man said. "I'm looking for fresh coriadner, not dried. It's for a garnish, after all."

"We don't sell fresh coriadner," Zešemkon said, without looking up from the counter.

"Really?" the man said. "Do you know who might sell it?"

"No," Zešemkon said.

"That's unfortunate," the man said. "Do you have any ideas what I could potentially use instead as a suitable garnish?"

Zešemkon thought for a moment. "I don't know," she said.

"Well, then," the man said. "I also couldn't find the Ñosng cheese. Where is that?"

"We don't sell that either," Zešemkon said.

"Oh dear," the man said. "Do you have Bliga?"

"No," Zešemkon said.

"How about Sudasichan oaea cheese?" the man asked.

"No," Zešemkon said.

"How about central Covtle melting cheese?" the man asked.

"No," Zešemkon said.

"Do you sell any soft cheeses at all?" the man asked.

"Olua, less-aged Tjanle, fleck cheese, and lograði cheese," Zešemkon said. "The refridgerator along the right wall, below the milk bottles."

"Thanks," the man said. He walked over to the right wall, checking the first several doors, before returning to the counter. "I don't see any Olua," he said. "Would you mind showing me where it is?"

"I can do that," Zešemkon said. She walked around from behind the counter and across the room over towards the refridgerators. She walked over to the second one, opened the door, and grabbed a bag of Olua cheese and handed it to the man. He smiled. "Thank you," he said.

Zešemkon took a moment to straighten and align the cheese bags and blocks so they were spaced evenly and in an exact row. She then closed the door and began to walk back towards the counter.

"One more thing," the man said. "I couldn't find any blister green apples. Do you have those or something similar?"

"We have lots of apples," Zešemkon said.

"Do you know which ones are tart?" he asked.

"All of them," Zešemkon.

"But which ones are as tart as the blister green?" the man asked.

"None of them," Zešemkon said.

The man sighed. "Do you know where I could find blister green apples?" he asked.

"At Lacaþblom ad laheave," Zešemkon said. "In the autumn."

"I'm looking for an apple now," the man said.

"I don't know which one you want," Zešemkon said. "I can only tell you where things are."

"I guess I'll check another shop then," the man said. He set the basket on the counter. "I'm ready to buy these ingredients."

Zešemkon glanced over the tin of tomato paste, the two red potatoes, the bottle of cream, the bag of salðol cheese, and the Olua cheese. She weighed the two potatoes, and entered the price of everything into the cash register. "5 zelums and 80 titoþa," she said.

The man got out a 5 zelum coin and a 1 zelum coin. He handed them to Zešemkon. Zešemkon clicked the button to open the cash drawer, placed the zelum coins in their slots, and handed the man a 20 titoþa coin. "Thank you," the man said.

Zešemkon handed the man his receipt. "Have a nice day," she said.

"You too," the man said. He gathered the ingredients into a bag, and walked out of the shop. Zešemkon waited a moment after the door had closed, and then walked over to a shelf full of tins. She began to straighten them, making sure they were aligned very precisely. After finishing the top shelf, she smiled slighly, and moved on to the shelf below.
